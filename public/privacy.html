Privacy Policy

This policy covers the SignalDeck website, self-hosted dashboard, and macOS companion app.

Last updated: April 3, 2026

The short version

SignalDeck does not sell your data, does not use third-party analytics, and does not collect personal information beyond what is strictly necessary to operate each product. The self-hosted dashboard and macOS app communicate exclusively with infrastructure you control.

1 Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to all products and services operated under the SignalDeck brand, including:

  • —The Website — the marketing and documentation site at signaldeck.tech
  • —The Dashboard — the self-hosted Laravel application you deploy on your own server
  • —The macOS App — the SignalDeck companion app distributed via the Mac App Store or direct download

The data controller for the website is Ben Wake / Wake&Co. For the self-hosted dashboard, you are the data controller — you own and operate the infrastructure.

2 The Website (signaldeck.tech)

The SignalDeck website is a static marketing site. We do not use first-party analytics, tracking pixels, or advertising scripts.

What our hosting provider may log: Standard web server access logs (IP address, browser user agent, pages visited, timestamps) may be retained by our hosting provider for operational and security purposes. We do not access or process these logs for marketing purposes.

Contact forms / email: If you contact us by email, we will use your email address solely to respond to your enquiry and will not add you to any mailing list without your explicit consent.

No cookies: The website does not set any persistent cookies.

3 The Self-Hosted Dashboard

The SignalDeck dashboard is open-source software that you install and operate on your own server. We have no access to your instance, your data, or your users.

When you self-host SignalDeck, you assume the role of data controller under applicable privacy law. The data your instance processes — user accounts, test run results, client records, authentication credentials — all resides on infrastructure you own and control.

What the software stores:

  • —User accounts (name, email address, hashed password or SSO tokens)
  • —Test run results, logs, and associated metadata
  • —Client and project records you create
  • —Session tokens and API keys (stored as hashed values)

None of this data is transmitted to us. The dashboard does not phone home, send telemetry, or contact any infrastructure we operate. The only outbound connections it makes are those you explicitly configure (e.g. SMTP for email, Slack webhooks).

As the operator, you are responsible for maintaining your own privacy policy for your end users and for complying with applicable data protection laws (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, etc.).

4 The macOS App

The SignalDeck macOS app is a native client that connects exclusively to a SignalDeck server URL that you configure. It does not relay, mirror, or forward any data to us or to any third-party service.

Data stored locally on your Mac:

  • —Server URL — stored in macOS application preferences
  • —Authentication credentials — API tokens or session credentials stored securely in the macOS Keychain using Apple's standard security APIs
  • —App preferences — notification settings and UI preferences stored in macOS UserDefaults

What we do not collect: We do not collect analytics, crash reports, device identifiers, usage telemetry, or any information about the server you connect to. There are no third-party SDKs embedded in the app that collect or transmit data.

Network communications: All outbound network activity is directed exclusively to the server URL you have configured. No other connections are made.

Authentication: The app supports username/password and SSO authentication. All authentication is handled between the app and your server. We have no visibility into your credentials, session state, or authentication flow.

5 Third-Party Services

SignalDeck does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or data-broker services across any of its products.

The website loads fonts from Google Fonts. This means Google's servers will receive your IP address when the page loads. You can prevent this by using a browser extension that blocks external font requests.

Source code is hosted on GitHub. Visiting the repository is subject to GitHub's Privacy Policy.

6 Children's Privacy

SignalDeck products are intended for professional software development teams. They are not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

7 Your Rights

Because we do not hold personal data about users of the dashboard or macOS app, there is nothing for us to provide, correct, delete, or transfer on request.

If your data is held by a self-hosted SignalDeck instance, any access, export, correction, or deletion request should be directed to the administrator of that instance — they are the data controller.

For any data associated with the website (e.g. an email you sent us), contact us at the address below and we will respond promptly.

8 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected here with a revised "Last updated" date. Continued use of any SignalDeck product after changes are published constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
